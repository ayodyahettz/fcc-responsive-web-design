Roald Dahl

  • 1916: Roald Dahl is born on the 13th of September 1916 in Cardiff, Wales to Norwegian parents Harald and Sophie Magdalene Dahl.

  • 1920: Roald's eldest sibling Astri dies from appendicitis at the age of seven. His father passes away from pneumonia shortly afterward. Roald was only three.

  • 1934: Roald crosses the Atlantic on the RMS Nova Scotia. He joined the Shell Petroleum Company.

  • 1936: Following two years of training with Shell, Dahl is assigned to Mombasa, Kenya, followed by Dar-es-Salaam, Tanganyika. His many experiences during this period are colourfully detailed in his autobiography written in his later years, 'Going Solo'.

  • 1939: In August, Dahl is commissioned into the King's African Rifles as a Lieutenant during the second world war. He was assigned to command indigenous troops that served for the British Empire.
    Roald joins the Royal AirForce as an aircraftman in November, his service number being 774022. It was after a 600-mile journey on car from Dar-es-Salaam to Nairobi that he was accepted for flight training with sixteen others. He flies a De Havilland Tiger Moth solo, with only seven hours and 40 minutes of experience. Of the seventeen trainees, only three survived the war.

  • 1940: Dahl is commissioned as a pilot officer, and was deemed eligible to join a squadron that faced the enemy.
    With no specialized training in aerial combat or flying obsolete Gloster Gladiators, Roald was ordered to fly with his squadron in stages. Unable to find the airstrip in the final leg while running low on fuel, he was forced to land on the desert, resulting in a crash. His skull gets fractured and nose is broken while being blinded temporarily. Luckily, he managed to get away from the wreckage before losing consciousness.
    He gets rescued and regains hsi consciousness, but not his eyesight. He is transported by train to Alexandria. It was later revealed that the location he was told to fly was wrong, and that he was mistakenly assigned to fly to the No Man's Land between the Italian and Allied forces.

  • 1941: Dahl is seen fit to return to his flying duties. His squadron had been transferred to the Greek campaign and was equipped with Hawker Hurricanes. He flies his first aerial combat mission solo on the 15th of April, bringing down bomber ships.

  • 1942: He is invalided home due to health reasons. He is later promoted to a war-substantive flight Lieutenant. He then worked with other well-known British officers, such as Ian Fleming, who later authored the James Bond series.

  • 1943: His first children's book, The Gremlins is published. It was based on mischievous creatures that were part of the RAF folklore.

  • 1946: The RAF invalidates Dahl from service after being pronounced unfit. He held the post of squadron leader, along with five aerial victories, qualifying him for a flying ace.

  • 1953: Roald marries American actress Patricia Neal.

  • 1960: Dahl'd son, Theo Dahl gets severely injured when his baby carriage was hit by a taxi at four months old. He suffered from hydrocephalus, and his father, Roald got involved in the development of the 'Wade-Dahl-Till' valve, a device used to alleviate the condition. It was later successfully used on thousands of children around the world.

  • 1962: Dahl's daughter Olivia dies at the age of seven due to measles encephalitis.

  • 1965: His wife, Patricia suffered from three burst cerebral aneurysms while pregnant with their fifth child, Lucy. She had to re-learn to walk and talk and managed to return to her acting career.

  • 1975: Dahl writes Danny, the Champion of the World..

  • 1983: Dahl divorces Patricia Neal and marries Felicity d'Abreu Crosland after an eleven-year relationship with her.

  • 1986: Dahl was offered an appointment to Officer of the Order of the British Empire (OBE) in the New Year's Honours list, but he turns it down.

  • 1990: Esio Trot is released in January, and becomes the last book published in his lifetime. Roald dies of myelodysplastic at the age of seventy four.

This article is based on Roald Dahl's Wikipedia entry and his autobiographies, Boy: Tales of Childhood and Going Solo.
